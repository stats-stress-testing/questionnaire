🔎 Purpose of this study
This survey is part of an academic research study examining the relationship between financial well-being and perceived stress among IT professionals in Sri Lanka. The study uses two internationally validated instruments — the CFPB Financial Well-Being Scale and the PSS-10 Perceived Stress Scale — to understand how financial circumstances may be associated with stress levels in this workforce. Findings will contribute to academic knowledge and may inform workplace well-being policies relevant to the Sri Lankan IT sector.
🔒 Confidentiality & data use
Your participation is completely voluntary and you may withdraw at any time without consequence. All responses are collected anonymously — no personally identifiable information is requested. Data will be stored securely, accessed only by the research team, and used solely for academic analysis and reporting. Aggregated results may be published in academic publications; individual responses will never be disclosed. By completing and submitting this survey you consent to your anonymised responses being used for these research purposes.
